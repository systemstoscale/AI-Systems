# Example Contexts

These are three filled-in examples showing how different users set up their context files. Use them as inspiration when filling in your own `business.md`, `personal.md`, and `strategy.md`.

---

## Example 1: Content Marketing Agency

### Business Context

- **Name**: Bright Ideas Media
- **What we do**: Content marketing agency that helps B2B SaaS companies attract and convert customers through high-quality written content.
- **Industry**: Content Marketing / Agency
- **Stage**: Growing (2 years in, profitable, expanding team)

**Services:**
- Blog Content Packages: Monthly retainers for SEO blog posts (4, 8, or 12 posts/month)
- Newsletter Management: End-to-end newsletter strategy, writing, and distribution
- Social Media Content: LinkedIn and Twitter/X content creation for founders and company pages
- Content Audits: One-time deep dives into existing content with actionable recommendations

**Audience:** B2B SaaS founders, marketing directors, and small marketing teams (10-100 employees). Their biggest pain point: they know content marketing works but don't have the time or consistency to do it well in-house.

**Brand Voice:** Conversational but credible. Sound like a smart colleague, not a textbook. Avoid corporate jargon, buzzwords like "synergy" or "leverage", overly salesy language.

**Tech Stack:** WordPress, ConvertKit, Ahrefs, Google Analytics, Notion, Canva, Buffer

### Personal Context

- **Name**: Alex Rivera
- **Role**: Content Director
- **Experience with code**: Intermediate — comfortable with HTML/CSS, can read Python, has used APIs
- **Communication style**: Brief and direct. Skip the preamble, get to the point.
- **Preferred output**: Markdown for drafts, plain text for social posts
- **Pet peeves**: No filler phrases ("In today's fast-paced world"), no generic intros, no padding

### Strategy

- **Top priority**: Launch weekly newsletter "The SaaS Content Playbook" — build owned audience for inbound leads
- **Goals**: 1,000 newsletter subscribers by Q2 (at 247), publish 3 blog posts/week (averaging 1.5), grow LinkedIn to 5,000 followers (at 2,100)
- **Not a priority**: Paid ads, YouTube, podcast, website redesign

## Example 2: University Student

### Business Context (Academic)

- **University**: State University
- **Department**: Computer Science (B.S.)
- **Focus area**: Distributed Systems
- **Courses**: CS 451 Distributed Systems, CS 380 Software Engineering, CS 340 Database Systems, MATH 310 Linear Algebra
- **Resources**: IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, Google Scholar, university library
- **Tools**: Overleaf (LaTeX), GitHub, Gradescope, Piazza

### Personal Context

- **Name**: Sam Park
- **Role**: Junior, CS major
- **Experience with code**: Intermediate (Python, Java; learning Go)
- **Communication style**: Step-by-step explanations preferred — wants the "why" behind each step
- **Preferred output**: Markdown files to `outputs/`. Final papers moved to Overleaf manually.
- **Risk tolerance**: Ask before destructive actions. Drafts and outlines are fine without asking.

### Strategy

- **Top priority**: Finish distributed systems research paper on consistency models in databases (3-week deadline)
- **Goals**: Complete research paper (3-week deadline), maintain 3.7+ GPA (currently 3.65), prepare for summer internship interviews (15 applications)
- **Not a priority**: Personal side projects, learning new languages, dev environment optimization

## Example 3: Management Consulting

### Business Context

- **Name**: Meridian Consulting
- **What we do**: Management consulting for mid-market companies — streamline operations, adopt new technology, manage organizational change.
- **Industry**: Management Consulting
- **Stage**: Established (5 years, 25 employees, ~$3M revenue)

**Services:**
- Process Optimization: Audit workflows, identify bottlenecks, redesign processes
- Digital Transformation: CRM migrations, workflow automation, data dashboards
- Change Management: Communication plans, training, adoption tracking

**Audience:** Mid-market companies (50-500 employees) in manufacturing, professional services, and healthcare. Pain point: internal processes built for a smaller company that now create friction.

**Tech Stack:** Google Workspace, HubSpot CRM, Slack, Asana, QuickBooks

### Personal Context

- **Name**: Jordan Chen
- **Role**: Operations Manager
- **Experience with code**: Beginner — comfortable with spreadsheets and formulas, no programming background
- **Communication style**: Detailed explanations with step-by-step guidance
- **Preferred output**: Markdown for internal docs, Google Slides-ready outlines for client presentations
- **Risk tolerance**: Ask before acting — especially anything touching client data or external communications

### Strategy

- **Top priority**: Reduce client onboarding from 3 weeks to 1 week
- **Goals**: Onboard 5 new clients this quarter, improve NPS to 85+ (currently 72), launch standardized reporting template
- **Key metrics**: Onboarding time 18 days (target 7), NPS 72 (target 85+), 12 active clients, ~$450K pipeline, 68% utilization (target 75%)
- **Not a priority**: Website redesign, hiring, building custom software
